robotics

making metal and plastic listen to me (eventually)

robotics is where my “what if we just automated that” brain gets to be loud. i like the mix of code, hardware, problem-solving, and the very specific joy of watching something physical move because of something i wrote.

stuff i’ve worked on

  • school robotics class projects, from basic bots to more cursed experiments
  • hands-on work with sensors, motors, and making everything not catch fire
  • tying robotics ideas into real-world things (like automation and retail systems)

what i like about it

  • you can actually see your code doing something in the real world
  • debugging is annoying but also kind of like solving a puzzle
  • it connects nicely with ai vision, automation, and my “let’s overengineer this” side

i fully believe robots should be slightly chaotic and full of personality. otherwise what’s the point.
